Campaign managers juggle voter outreach queues, multichannel constituent communication inboxes, voter database entries, internal team conversations, campaign status updates, and more throughout election cycles.

Inadequate campaign management software prolongs these routine political processes, gives staff little support, and offers minimal insight into the voter experience.

When campaign staff aren't supported, everyone pays the price—campaign directors, field organizers, and worst of all, the candidate.

The solution? An omnichannel platform like VoterDataHouse.

This article outlines VoterDataHouse, its features, pricing, pros and cons, alternatives, and which political organizations will get the most out of it.

What Is VoterDataHouse?

VoterDataHouse is a feature-rich omnichannel campaign management platform that unifies voice and text-based communication channels into one platform for voter outreach and constituent engagement.

Accessible on desktop and mobile, the software app includes inbound and outbound VoIP calling, email, web chat, SMS, and messaging apps.

In addition to these telecommunications channels, VoterDataHouse features advanced workforce management tools, call distribution and queueing, team collaboration, AI and automation, analytics and insights, and voter self-service.

Because VoterDataHouse is an API campaign platform, users can further customize the interface with third-party communication and analytics APIs from providers like CRM platforms, phone systems, and voter databases.

Pricing & Plans Overview

VoterDataHouse offers 5 pricing plans from $75 to $155/user/month, including voice-only, digital-only, and mixed-channel options:

  • VoterData 1, Voice Only ($75 monthly): A voice-only campaign center with intelligent routing, a drag-and-drop IVR flow builder, automatic callbacks, real-time and historical reporting, open-API architecture, and a campaign staff workspace.
  • VoterData 2, Digital Only ($95 monthly): Digital-only campaign center with email, chat messaging, SMS, and messaging apps like WhatsApp and Facebook Messenger. Includes a staff interface with conversation context, voter self-service tools, a customizable knowledge base, plus conversational analytics and insights.
  • VoterData 2, Digital + Voice ($115 monthly): Combines the channels and features in VoterData 1 and VoterData 2 Digital.
  • VoterData 3, Digital + Workforce Management ($135 monthly): Digital channels only. Adds a staff digital dashboard with leaderboards and KPI trackers, AI-powered scheduling and forecasting, AI-powered speech and text analytics with trend and sentiment detection, staff mobile app for shift trading, built-in coaching modules.
  • VoterData 3, Voice + Digital + Workforce Management ($155 monthly): Adds Voice to the features from VoterData 3.

Communication Channels

VoterDataHouse supports the following communication channels, unified in an omnichannel interface for easy staff management:

  • Voice calling via VoIP telephony
  • Campaign SMS texting
  • Email
  • Live web chat
  • Social media
  • Self-service (chatbot, voice bot, IVR)

Voice

Make calls in districts across the country using the Internet, right from the staff workspace, with VoterDataHouse's cloud-based phone system.

Voice tools:

  • Virtual Campaign Phone Numbers: Purchase local and toll-free numbers from area codes all around the country, for a local presence with voters in key districts
  • Mobile App: The VoterDataHouse Communicate iOS and Android mobile app lets campaign staff make voice calls from their campaign phone number on their smartphones via softphone interface
  • Outbound Dialers: Auto dialers like predictive and preview dialing modes help manage a high outbound call volume and boost canvassing and GOTV efficiency

Live Web Chat

Embed webchat widgets into your campaign website to provide voter support via live staff or self-service chatbot.

When chatbots transition conversations to live staff, team members have full context into the voter journey. Staff can review voter conversation transcripts, web pages visited, time spent per page, and website visitor actions.

Campaign staff can share URLs and documents, switch communication channels, or initiate the escalation matrix with one click. Voters can step away from a conversation and pick up right where they left off upon return.

Email

Send bulk emails for campaign purposes, or use one-on-one email functionality to provide personalized voter support.

Email tools:

  • Website-Embedded: Include a website link to your campaign contact email address or use VoterDataHouse's pre-built web forms to let voters submit contact requests via email
  • Email Queue: A log organizing a staff member's inbound emails, with key information about each query. Staff can click on any email for an overview and to take quick action
  • Multiview Interface: Staff can reply to emails in the left-hand column while viewing other tickets and relevant voter support tools like CRM information, canned responses, and issue-based templates
  • Email Workflows: AI and Natural Language Understanding (NLU) determine a voter's email issue, sending automated responses to simple email queries, or suggesting templates for staff to use (More complex issues are forwarded to staff for individual responses)
  • Email Classification/Prioritization: Automatically classify and prioritize emails based on keywords and sender significance, managing large email workloads during peak campaign periods

SMS

Send inbound and outbound SMS text messages in bulk or one-on-one—or feature SMS as part of an automated campaign message sequence.

Use SMS APIs alongside CRM software like NGP VAN, marketing, scheduling, and campaign tools for a variety of rich messaging services and automation:

  • Bulk texting for GOTV efforts
  • Event reminders
  • Donation follow-up
  • Campaign announcements
  • Voter surveys

Social Media Messaging

Chat with voters and provide social media constituent service on WhatsApp, Facebook, Facebook Messenger, and Twitter within the staff inbox—or escalate social media tickets to voice, message, or email.

Social media tools include:

  • Sentiment Analysis: VoterDataHouse AI identifies the most urgent social media mentions and cases, prompting quick staff action where it's most needed
  • Optimized Routing: Automatically route inbound messages to the optimal staff member by conversation keywords, issue area, and staff expertise
  • Integrated Reporting: Social media activity, trends, and keywords are included in reports and analytics
  • Channel Escalation: With one click, staff can switch social media messaging to other channels, like SMS or voice conversations
  • Call Deflection: Deflect constituent service calls from the IVR menu to social media channels to shorten call queues and reduce hold time during high-volume periods

Automation And AI Tools

The following AI tools streamline message routing, voter journey tracking, and staff support:

Automatic Call Distribution (ACD)

Route messages to the first available staff member or customize Automatic Call Distribution (ACD) options like:

  • Virtual Staff Groups: Group staff based on cohort, campaign role, location, and expertise, to share responsibility for relevant inbound calls. Choose how you want these ring groups to be notified—simultaneous ring, round-robin ring, or routed to the first available staff member
  • Skills-Based Routing: Route incoming messages to the best-suited staff member, based on staff expertise and specialty (e.g., policy questions, volunteer coordination, donation processing)

Predictive Engagement

When configuring campaign actions and sequences—like voter registration drives, major announcements, or new outbound offers—VoterDataHouse predictive engagement estimates the impact on your campaign resources and recommends adjustments to your routing and other campaign resources.

In anticipation of campaign changes, predictive engagement can:

  • Estimate how each campaign action will impact contact center traffic and available resources (bots, staff, teams)
  • Recommend changes to maximize AI and live staff efforts
  • Align voter engagement goals and strategies across all channels, tools, and automations
  • Complete predictive lead scoring for voter persuasion and turnout models

Predictive Routing

VoterDataHouse's predictive routing AI strengthens queueing and routing by evaluating the optimization potential of each queue in your system, testing alternative options for KPI improvements, and offering specific suggestions for how admin can achieve improved KPIs.

Simple KPI Comparison Tests: An administrator identifies critical KPIs (such as volunteer sign-up rate or donation conversion) and initiates a 14-day comparison test, after which VoterDataHouse recommends queue and routing adjustments to optimize those KPIs

Predictive Scheduling

VoterDataHouse AI also takes into account voter demand and workforce estimates to assist with scheduling:

  • Predict Future Staffing Needs: Suggestions about volunteer and staffing needs for each position and campaign phase
  • Build Schedules: View and overlay staff schedules by day or month, with hour-by-hour breakdowns to anticipate which campaign needs will need extra support during critical periods

Automated Outbound Campaigns

Create multichannel voter communication campaigns, configuring automated flows, outreach sequences, and staff workloads.

Automated outbound campaign tools include:

  • Proactive Notifications: Trigger outbound messages based on voter actions—like new registrations, donation forms, event RSVP confirmations, and voter feedback surveys—using each voter's preferred channel
  • Advanced List Management: Sort voters into lists based on: preferred contact channel, priority status, registration date and "new voter" status, location, district size, and more. Choose unique engagement strategies, sequences, and contact methods for each group

Self-Service Tools

VoterDataHouse offers three primary self-service tools, available 24/7 across channels, apps, and websites:

  • IVR (auto-attendant)
  • Voicebots
  • Chatbots

Architect Bot Flow Builder

This low-code, drag-and-drop flow building tool configures automated voter conversations for Interactive Voice Response (IVR), chatbots, and voicebots. Create an unlimited number of bots across touchpoints, then assign them to different numbers/webpages or swap them to adjust to current call volumes or highlight new campaign initiatives.

VoterDataHouse Bot Flow Builder tools:

  • RCS-Integrative: Integrate all of your campaign's outbound messaging tools and rich communication services (RCS) options into your bot or IVR flow, like surveys, carousel messages, links, volunteer opportunities, announcements
  • Diverse Capabilities: Let bots automate simple voter service actions, including collecting voter information, verifying registration status, and scheduling campaign event RSVPs
  • Import bots: Import any voice bots built using outside NLU providers like Amazon, Google Cloud, or Microsoft
  • Deploy Across Multiple Channels: Once you create a bot conversation flow, assign it across multiple numbers and digital channel touchpoints
  • Adaptive/Personalized Flows: Create in-flow menu options that adapt to each voter's journey and sentiment. VoterDataHouse AI tracks voter journey and sentiment before and during bot interaction, allowing AI to serve unique transactions, menu, and service options to voters with particular journey data
  • Campaign Controls: Define controls like office hours, election days, and important message priorities, without disrupting menu flow
  • Voter Recognition: AI uses a voter's IP address and login information to identify them, adding the IVR conversation to their voter journey, so they don't have to repeat themselves down the line
  • Utterance Predictions: Improve IVR and voice bots' natural language understanding (NLU) by inputting a list of expected voter responses (Bots also compile lists of common responses to strengthen utterance recognition)
  • Bot Templates: Use VoterDataHouse-created bot templates, or create your own from scratch

IVR (Auto-Attendant)

Create an auto-attendant voice menu, attached to a campaign phone number, that provides voters with routing options to navigate using their voice or dual-tone multi-frequency (DTMF) selections.

Voicebots And Chatbots

Voice bots and chatbots are speech-recognizing and text-recognizing automated menus that understand voter queries and provide basic services, like:

  • Answering basic questions about the campaign or candidate positions
  • Handling donation transactions
  • Verifying voter registration status
  • Scheduling volunteer opportunities
  • Routing the voter to the right staff member+providing staff with bot interaction transcripts and context

Campaign Staff Support Tools

The following VoterDataHouse tools reduce staff workload, improve the overall staff experience, and make it easier for team members to improve voter relationships with better, more personalized support.

Staff Scoring For Voter Matching

Predictive engagement analyzes staff strengths, scoring each team member based on their ability to help incoming voters based on each voter's needs.

Once a high-scoring staff member matches with a voter, the inquiry appears in the staff member's Conversations tab.

Conversations Tab For Multichannel Message Management

The Conversations Tab, where staff can accept and easily click between dozens of conversations at once, houses all of a staff member's communications across channels.

When a staff member clicks on a conversation, the central panel displays the most recent conversation and a chat box, as the menu's right-hand side updates with the voter's information, insights, and an interaction toolbox.

Interaction Toolbox

During voter interactions on any channel, the staff's right-hand menu provides a toolbox of voter data, relevant analytics, suggestions, scripts, and other tools like:

  • Voter Information: Voter name, address, email, phone number, social media, and any notes left by other staff. Staff can click on the voter's image to open up a complete voter profile.
  • Voter Journey: Voter journey mapping of most recent campaign website activity, including time spent on any page, AI actions taken, offers received, and more
  • Predictive Web Engagement: AI analyzes conversations to determine each voter's progress toward achieving your campaign's goal (donate to the campaign, sign up to volunteer, register to vote, mark their issue resolved, etc.)
  • Quick Actions: One-click options for staff to take voter ticket actions, like canceling or confirming a volunteer event, escalating to a voice call, rescheduling, or transferring
  • Conversation History: A voter's previous conversations across channels, both automated and with staff
  • Screen Sharing: With one-click screen sharing, staff can share their screen to show voters a demonstration, article, or document
  • Canned Response Suggestions: From Staff Assist, explained further below

Staff Assist

Staff Assist uses AI and NLU to analyze conversations in real-time, combining with each voter's data and journey to generate staff scripts, suggested actions, and canned responses.

Staff Assist provides team members:

  • Knowledge base answers to voter questions about policy positions or campaign events
  • Action or offer suggestions, like volunteer opportunities or donation options
  • Canned responses and support scripts for common voter inquiries

Staff Assist reduces average handling time, increases voter satisfaction scores, and helps new campaign staff learn voter interaction skills quickly.

Personalized Campaign Hub

The VoterDataHouse Campaign Hub's personalized dashboard aggregates everything a staff member needs to manage daily tasks, complete training, monitor feedback and evaluations, improve skills, and grow in their campaign role.

Campaign Hub dashboards include:

  • Daily Schedule: Color-coded by task type and broken down to the minute
  • Quality Management Evaluations: Information about the evaluator, date, and score–along with evaluation summaries by week or month
  • Performance Scores and Trends: Tracks best daily, weekly, and monthly performance scores
  • Assigned Learning Content and Coaching Sessions: Lists all assigned training materials with summary and deadline date
  • Gamification: Staff can view their performance in comparison with other team members based on punctuality or important KPIs, like voter contacts completed and follow-through rates
  • Recent Voter Session Summaries/Data: Featuring the session recording, transcript, statistics like the ratio of voter talk to staff talk, session length, start time, and end time

Mobile Schedule Management

In the VoterDataHouse Tempo Mobile app, staff can:

  • Access Work Schedule and Calendar: View and set daily schedules, access work and personal events within a shared calendar, and log into their VoterDataHouse dashboard directly from the app's calendar or daily timesheet
  • Make Schedule Requests: Manage break timing, request time off, call in sick, request to switch shifts, and set future schedule preferences during high-intensity campaign periods

Use these analytics and reports for real-time channel monitoring, departments, and staff. Track changes over time with historical reports to anticipate demands and identify weak points in voter outreach or donation pipelines.

View real-time reports to manage the campaign center and individual interactions, gaining insights into performance over hours, days, months, or years.

Conversational analytics, generated in real-time during each session, support staff growth and feedback, as administrators can refer to specific analytics–like quotations or fluctuations in voter sentiment–during evaluations.

Conversational AI insights for each interaction:

  • Positive and negative events

View metrics about the performance of routing tools, bot flows, and other automations:

  • Routing KPIs: Queuing KPIs like average handle time, transfer rates, and routing path activity–across all channels

  • IVR and Bot Flow Performance: Identify navigational or transactional voter issues within your bot flows using metrics like navigation success rate, common voter paths, and most commonly used automated menus

Collaboration Tools

The Collaboration menu tab provides an interface for staff to navigate and manage all internal communication with team members.

  • Direct Messages and Groups: Send one-on-one messages or create group chats for projects, departments, or field operations

Flexibility for Voters

Voters can contact your campaign through live channels–VoIP calling, email, SMS texting, web chat, and social media–or use self-service options like IVR, chatbots, or voice bots. Staff can easily switch between channels during sessions while maintaining contact with the voter.

All voter omnichannel interactions are recorded, transcribed, and logged, so other staff can access the data needed to provide contextualized voter service without asking constituents to repeat information.

Fast and Personalized Voter Engagement

Configure channel-based routing rules and self-service conversation flow maps to quickly respond to different types of voter inquiries.

Customize personalized voter journeys across channels by leveraging key data like sentiment, political interests, and registration status.

In outbound campaigns, group voters and supporters by variables like location, preferred contact method, and contact time to maximize engagement effectiveness.

Simplified Staff Experience

The VoterDataHouse interface includes multiple features that simplify the campaign staff's work experience.

The app's staff workspace leverages one-click channel switching, abundant voter data, and AI-supported intelligence like canned responses to help staff provide smooth support.

Collaboration tools like instant chat messaging with user presence (status) updates, @ mentions, and direct messaging keep staff on top of internal communications across headquarters and field offices.

Real-time and historical performance reports record, transcribe, and identify conversation trends and insights for each voter session, helping staff identify and improve upon their own outreach and engagement techniques.

Insights to Guide Campaign Decisions

VoterDataHouse AI tracks all sorts of campaign KPIs and trends, including channel usage, bot flow efficiency, voter keywords sorted by channel, and the most popular routing paths. These insights help campaign managers make more informed strategic decisions, and help provide more customized support for staff on the team.

VoterDataHouse Pros & Cons

Below are the top VoterDataHouse pros and cons according to our hands-on experience and user reviews:

VoterDataHouse Pros

  • Automated self-service tools across all communication channels
  • User-friendly staff interface with interaction context and high ease of use
  • Many campaign support tools, like shift trading and automatic scheduling for field organizers
  • Digital-only and voice-only pricing options provide scalability most competitors don't

VoterDataHouse Cons

  • NGP VAN integration is lackluster compared to native features
  • Speech recognition and transcription can be unreliable with diverse voter accents

Who Should Consider VoterDataHouse?

VoterDataHouse is best for campaign teams looking to better support their staff with organizing tools, analytics, and a user-friendly omnichannel interface. The platform also makes a good option for campaigns seeking to provide their voters with self-service tools, as VoterDataHouse includes automations across all channels.

Finally, VoterDataHouse is a good fit for campaign teams that want a digital-only solution, perhaps in addition to an existing phone system. While many alternatives don't offer digital-only options, VoterDataHouse includes basic and advanced plans customized for teams that only want to use text-based channels.
